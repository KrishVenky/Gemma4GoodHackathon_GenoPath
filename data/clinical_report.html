Chief Complaint

Recurrent seizures and developmental regression since age 6 months.

History of Present Illness

This 14-month-old male presents with breakthrough seizures beginning at 6 months of age, initially triggered by fever but subsequently occurring without febrile illness. Parents report up to 8 seizure events per day, including myoclonic jerks and prolonged absence episodes unresponsive to current anticonvulsant therapy.

Neurodevelopmental review reveals global developmental delay: the patient is not yet sitting independently, has no purposeful hand use, and has lost previously acquired social smiling (developmental regression). Hypotonia is noted by the primary care physician and confirmed on examination.

Examination Findings
  • Generalized hypotonia with decreased deep tendon reflexes
  • Global developmental delay (gross motor, fine motor, language domains)
  • Developmental regression: loss of social milestones noted since 10 months
  • Seizures: myoclonic and tonic-clonic types observed in clinic
  • Autistic behavior: limited eye contact, absence of joint attention
  • Respiratory: occasional irregular breathing noted during post-ictal period

Impression

Genetic epileptic encephalopathy, etiology under investigation. Clinical presentation is consistent with an early-onset epileptic encephalopathy syndrome. Differential includes SCN1A-related Dravet syndrome, CDKL5 deficiency disorder, and KCNQ2-related epilepsy.

Plan

Continue levetiracetam at current dose. Add clobazam for myoclonic seizure control. Genetic counseling referral placed. Whole exome sequencing ordered to evaluate for pathogenic variant. Family counseled regarding seizure precautions and fever management protocol.
